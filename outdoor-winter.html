Winter Sports

Hit the slopes or glide across the ice in Germany's top winter destinations.

Winter Sports in Germany

Germany might not be the first country that comes to mind for winter sports, but it has some excellent skiing, snowboarding, and winter activities — especially in the Bavarian Alps, which receive reliable snowfall from November through April. Beyond skiing, Germany's winter season is also filled with magical Christmas markets, ice skating rinks, and cosy mountain huts.

Top Winter Sports Destinations

Garmisch-Partenkirchen

Germany's premier ski resort, located at the foot of the Zugspitze. Host of the 1936 Winter Olympics with over 40 km of ski runs.

Berchtesgaden

A charming Alpine town near Salzburg with ski areas, toboggan runs, and stunning mountain scenery in the Berchtesgaden Alps.

Oberstdorf

Germany's southernmost town and a top winter sports hub. Offers skiing, cross-country trails, and is home to a famous ski jumping stadium.

Fichtelgebirge (Franconia)

A lower mountain range in northern Bavaria with popular family-friendly ski areas and extensive cross-country skiing trails.

Harz Mountains

Central Germany's best option for winter sports, with downhill and cross-country skiing around the Brocken summit.

Black Forest

The Feldberg area in the southern Black Forest is a popular winter destination, with ski runs suitable for beginners and families.

Winter Travel Tip

Winter is also the perfect time to explore Germany's famous Christmas markets. Combine a ski trip to Bavaria with a visit to the Nuremberg or Munich Christmas market for an unforgettable winter experience.
