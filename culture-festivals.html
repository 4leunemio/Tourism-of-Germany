Festivals

Celebrate Germany's rich traditions and lively festivals all year round.

Germany's Famous Festivals

Germany is a country that loves to celebrate. Throughout the year, towns and cities across the country host festivals dedicated to beer, food, music, history, and seasonal traditions. Whether you visit in summer or winter, there is always something to enjoy.

Top Festivals

Oktoberfest — Munich

The world's most famous beer festival. Held every year from late September to early October in Munich. Over 6 million visitors attend each year. Features enormous beer tents, traditional Bavarian food, and live music.

Christmas Markets

Germany's Christmas markets (Weihnachtsmärkte) are magical winter events held in city squares from late November. Nuremberg, Cologne, and Dresden have some of the most beautiful ones.

Karneval (Carnival)

Germany's wild pre-Lent carnival. Cologne's Karneval is particularly famous, with street parades, costumes, and music starting on the Thursday before Ash Wednesday.

Rhine in Flames

A spectacular series of firework displays along the Rhine River, held on five summer evenings between May and September.

Berlinale

One of the world's most prestigious film festivals, held in Berlin every February. It attracts major international film stars and directors.

Stuttgart Wine Festival

One of the largest open-air wine festivals in the world, held each August in Stuttgart's market square with over 500 wines on offer.

Festival Calendar

  • February — Karneval (Cologne, Düsseldorf, Mainz)
  • February — Berlinale International Film Festival (Berlin)
  • May–September — Rhine in Flames fireworks events
  • August — Stuttgart Wine Festival
  • Late September to October — Oktoberfest (Munich)
  • November to December — Christmas Markets across all major cities
