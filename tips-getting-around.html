Getting Around Germany

Germany has one of Europe's best transport networks — navigating it is easy.

Getting Around Germany

Germany is one of the easiest countries in Europe to travel around. Its extensive rail, bus, and road networks mean you can reach almost any destination efficiently and comfortably. Whether you prefer to travel by train, car, bus, or even bicycle, Germany has excellent options for every traveller.


By Train (Deutsche Bahn)

The Deutsche Bahn (DB) is Germany's national railway operator and runs one of Europe's most comprehensive rail networks. Trains are generally punctual, comfortable, and a very pleasant way to travel.

  • ICE (Intercity-Express) — Germany's high-speed trains, travelling up to 300 km/h between major cities.
  • IC/EC (Intercity/Eurocity) — Fast intercity trains connecting cities not served by the ICE network.
  • Regional trains (RE/RB) — Slower trains that stop at smaller towns and villages. Usually covered by local transport passes.
  • S-Bahn — Suburban city rail networks in large cities like Munich, Berlin, Hamburg, and Frankfurt.

By Car

Germany has an excellent motorway (Autobahn) network. Note that while many sections have no speed limit, many others do — always check the signs. A Green Environmental Badge (Umweltplakette) is required to enter most city centre low-emission zones.

  • Rental cars are available at all airports and major cities
  • Traffic drives on the right side of the road
  • Road signs are in German — a GPS or map app is recommended
  • Parking in city centres can be expensive — park-and-ride options are available

By Bus

Long-distance buses (FlixBus, Blablabus) are a budget-friendly alternative to trains, especially for routes not well-covered by rail. They are slower but significantly cheaper for last-minute bookings.


In Cities

All major German cities have excellent public transport networks including U-Bahn (subway), S-Bahn (city rail), trams, and buses. Day passes and weekly passes offer good value for tourists.


Germany Ticket (Deutschlandticket)

The Deutschlandticket is a monthly flat-rate ticket that allows unlimited travel on all regional trains, S-Bahn, U-Bahn, buses, and trams across all of Germany. It is outstanding value for tourists spending a week or more in the country.
