Hiking in Germany

Explore Germany's stunning trails, mountains, and national parks on foot.

Hiking in Germany

Germany offers some of Europe's most beautiful and diverse hiking landscapes. From the rugged granite peaks of the Bavarian Alps to the densely forested hills of the Black Forest and the dramatic cliff paths of the Saxon Switzerland National Park, there is a trail for every level of hiker.

Germany has over 200,000 km of marked hiking trails, and the national trail network is exceptionally well organised and signposted. Most trails are free to use and many pass through charming villages, historic ruins, and scenic viewpoints.

Best Hiking Regions

Bavarian Alps

Home to Germany's highest peak, the Zugspitze (2,962 m). Trails range from easy valley walks to challenging alpine routes.

Black Forest (Schwarzwald)

Dense pine forests, waterfalls, and rolling hills in southwest Germany. The Westweg trail stretches 285 km from north to south.

Saxon Switzerland

Dramatic sandstone rock formations near Dresden. The Malerweg (Painter's Way) is one of Germany's most scenic long-distance trails.

Harz Mountains

Central Germany's highest mountain range. The Brocken (1,141 m) is a popular summit hike with a historic railway to the top.

Rügen Island (Baltic Coast)

Chalk cliffs, beech forests, and coastal trails along Germany's largest island — a beautiful and peaceful walking destination.

Eifel National Park

Germany's youngest national park in the Rhineland, with volcanic lakes, ancient forests, and excellent well-marked trail networks.

Hiking Tips

Germany's trails are very well signposted. Always carry water, a trail map, and appropriate footwear. Mountain rescue services (Bergwacht) operate across alpine regions. Check weather forecasts before heading into higher elevations.
