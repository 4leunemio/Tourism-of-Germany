Cycling in Germany

Germany is one of Europe's top cycling destinations with thousands of km of routes.

Cycling in Germany

Germany is a paradise for cyclists. The country has over 70,000 km of dedicated cycle paths, and cycling culture is deeply embedded in everyday German life. From leisurely riverside routes to challenging mountain passes, there is something for every type of cyclist.

Long-distance cycle routes (Fernradwege) connect cities, regions, and even different countries. Many follow rivers like the Rhine, Elbe, Danube, and Moselle, offering flat and scenic journeys through wine villages, historic towns, and beautiful countryside.

Top Cycling Routes

Rhine Cycle Route (EuroVelo 15)

Follow the Rhine from its source in Switzerland to the Netherlands. The German section passes through the romantic Rhine Valley gorge.

Danube Cycle Path

One of Europe's most popular cycle routes. The German section runs from the Black Forest to Passau, through beautiful Bavarian towns.

Elbe Cycle Route

A 1,260 km route following the Elbe River from the Czech border through Dresden, Magdeburg, and Hamburg to the North Sea.

Berlin to Copenhagen

A long-distance tour from Berlin through the north German lowlands and across Denmark to Copenhagen — approximately 630 km.

Moselle Wine Route

Cycle through Germany's most beautiful wine region, past vine-covered slopes, medieval castles, and charming wine villages.

Baltic Sea Cycle Route

Follow the German Baltic coastline from Flensburg to Ahlbeck, passing sandy beaches, fishing villages, and cliff landscapes.

Bike Rental

Bikes can be rented in most German cities and tourist areas. The DB (Deutsche Bahn) also allows bicycles on most regional trains, making it easy to combine cycling with rail travel. Call a Bike (DB's bike-sharing service) operates in many major cities.
