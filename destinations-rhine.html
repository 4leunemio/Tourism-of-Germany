Rhine Valley

Enjoy scenic river cruises past iconic medieval castles perched above lush, vine-covered hillsides. Afterward, savor world-class wines directly from the region's legendary riverside vineyards.

About the Rhine Valley

The Middle Rhine Valley is one of Germany's most picturesque regions and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Stretching between the cities of Bingen and Koblenz, the Rhine Gorge is lined with steep vineyard slopes, medieval castles, and charming riverside villages.

The valley is famous for its Riesling wines, which thrive on the steep, slate-covered slopes. A river cruise along the Rhine is one of the most iconic travel experiences in all of Germany, passing dozens of historic castles and fortresses along the way.



Top Attractions

Loreley Rock

A legendary slate rock overlooking the Rhine — one of the most visited natural landmarks in Germany.

Rheinfels Castle

The largest ruin on the Middle Rhine, overlooking the town of St. Goar. Stunning views over the valley.

Rhine River Cruise

A classic boat trip along the Rhine is the best way to take in the castles, vineyards, and villages.

Things to Do in the Rhine Valley

  • Take a Rhine River cruise from Bingen to Koblenz
  • Visit the Loreley rock formation and viewpoint
  • Explore Rheinfels Castle and its extensive ruins
  • Wine tasting in the Rüdesheim wine region
  • Hike the RheinBurgenWeg trail along the river gorge
  • Visit the charming wine village of Bacharach
  • Explore Koblenz and the Deutsches Eck (German Corner)
